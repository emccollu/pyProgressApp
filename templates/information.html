Information on Degree Progress Generator

The generated results are by no means definitive, though they are as close as possible.
Every department has specific requirements, and every degree has another set of requirements.
Also, all requirements can change, and the ones for your degree are based on the year you began.
The generator's results for grade calulations will be accurate. The results for majors an minors are based on the current catalog, and represent your status if you signed up for them in this catalog year.
For now, here is some information on how your degree progress is determined, based on the UNLV course catalog and school's website.
Links will be provided to where this information was posted by the school.

If you notice anything incorrect or missing, please feel free to contact me.

Overall

  • Total credits to graduate must be at least 120.
  • General Requirements are required for every student, though often they are covered by the require courses for that major.
  • Upper Division courses are typically meant as level 300+, in a few cases it's 400+.
  • Above that, 500+ level courses, are for a masters degree and not covered here.

Grades

The generator will display the courses that have the most impact on your grade.
You might think "obviously it's the lowest grade," but its a combination of grades and credits.
For example, a D in a 3 credit course has the same impact on your GPA as a C in a 4 credit course.
The details page will show a list of all your grades in order of their amount of negative impact.
Some courses have a limit on the number of attempts, and if you retake it after that then both grades will effect your gpa.
